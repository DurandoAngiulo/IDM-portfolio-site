Redesigning Techradar.com

For my 2nd official UI project, I was tasked with analyzing an existing news website and employ heuristic analysis to identify its weaknesses. I then was charged with using the mobile-first design philosophy to redesign the site at the three major breakpoints(Mobile, Tablet, & Desktop) with advertisement content factored inFor this assignment, I chose Techradar.com because I felt that my personality made up a good portion of the user base and that it would be easy to come up with an effective site redesign.

ANALYSIS

Part I

Mapping the Site

My first step in this process was to “map out” the current organization of Techradar.com. I accomplished this by:

  • Tracing over screenshots of of the actual site
    • Cataloging different image and written content as well as ads.
The goal of this exercise was to gain insights and perspective on the site layout as it currently stands now.

Identifying the User

From Techradar’s advertisement media kit, I was able to cultivate an idea of the site’s current audience and constructed a preliminary user persona so I could get a better sense of who I was redesigning the site for. Through my research, I determined that my average user was:

  • Young to Middle age
  • College Educated
  • Had higher incomes
  • Technologically affluent
  • Intelligent

Pinpointing Weaknesses

Next, I set out to identify the key problems within Techradar.com’s design. These factors were based on a heuristic analysis I performed on the site, which fixated on identifying usability issues that hampered the current site design. The main problems I identified as a result of this assessment were that:

  • Ads were sometimes intrusive and were often put first over content, often taking away from the actual site
  • The Navigational menu wasn’t intuitive which made navigating the actual site cumbersome
  • The site lacked any Help and Feedback pages/forms
  • The visual design was much to be desired and overall “uninspiring” or engaging to users.

Laying out my Goals

After finishing my initial breakdown of Techradar.com, I laid out my goals that I would focus on in my redesign. My main focuses included:

  • Fixing navigation so that it is never hard or cumbersome to navigate the actual site.
  • Changing advertisement placement/format so that ads are no longer intrusive or dominating over actual site content
  • Overhauling the visual design to give the site a fresh look that engages audiences and reflects Techradar’s brand

DESIGN

Part II

Sketching out Ideas

My first step in designing my actual site again revolved around rough wireframe sketches. These drawings weren’t about detail and their primary goal revolved around me getting my initial layout ideas onto a physical space.

Refining my Vision

From the previous sketches, I took the design I felt was the strongest of my ideations and tried to refine it as best I could. the design I ended choosing was sharp and futuristic looking. While some of the content organization stayed the same, if shifted other elements around to fit the new look I was going for. Within the visual design itself, there weren’t really soft angles and every piece of image content had its own hard shadow behind it, whose goal as to make the image itself pop. While these sketches were far from the completed redesign I wanted, they served as important groundwork for me to build off of in the design process.

Finding my Colors

Next, I consolidated a color palette that would be used in the creation of my redesign. I personally thought Techradar’s current choice of colors were mostly bland and uninspiring.So, while selecting a new palette I tried to shift the color scheme of the existing brand, while not abandoning the original colors completely. For instance, while all the blues I used in my redesign were a shift from the original theme, I kept the signature bold pink from the original colors and integrated it into my new color palette.

Exploring Typography and Iconography

After color selection, I then played around with different font and icon types. For this project, I again moved away from Techradar.com’s current font and icon layout and instead tried to find alternative font types that fit the new design aesthetics I was going to implement in the redesign.

Building site Elements

Using the web prototyping software Figma, I begin building the elements that would make up my site redesign. I did this using atomic design techniques starting with “atom” and “molecule” elements working my way up to building “organisms” or groupings of atoms and molecules. This design workflow helped me keep all my site elements organized and made it easy for me to make edits to the structure of of my site.

Crafting High-Res Wireframes

After I completed all my style-based information and built the framework of my redesign, I created my High-Res wireframes in Figma, putting together all the work I had done in my earlier steps. These wireframes would ultimately be used as the template to polish my final High-Res interactive Prototype and introduce interactivity.

FINAL HIGH-RES PROTOTYPE

Part III

My Final Design

From my high-res wireframes, I populated my site redesign with actual Techradar.com content/advertisements and added multiple types of interactivity to demo at all breakpoints, such as:

  • Dropdown Menus/tabs
  • Working Naviagation
  • Image Carousels
  • Hover Interactivity

Final thoughts

I was very happy how my final site redesign turned out. At the very least, I believe I addressed all the heuristic problems present in Techradar.com’s current design. Using new skills I was introduced to during the design process and by leaning on things I learned in my past projects, I felt that I made constructive and effective decisions that gave my final redesign a fresh new perspective on what Techradar.com could potentially look like. Of course my redesign itself isn’t perfect; I personally believe nothing you ever design can be truly perfect. But through this project I can see that my skills and design instincts are growing. I still have a lot of learning to do to become better at UI Design, but its nice to see how i’ve improved during the course of this assignment.
