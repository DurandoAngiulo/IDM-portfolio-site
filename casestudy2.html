Rebranding Nord VPN

For my final UX/UI project of my Freshman year, I was assigned with picking an existing app I knew well and mapping out it's interaction flow. Then, I was tasked with completely rebranding my chosen app with a new name/logo and with a newly designed/animated interaction flow that highlights it's primary purpose. I chose Nord VPN for my app because I use it often as a user and know the ins and outs of the interface very well.

ANALYSIS

Part I

Mapping Nord's Task Flow

The first thing I did beginning this process was mapping the current sequence a user takes in order to complete Nord's primary task(In this case my task was to connect to a VPN Server). Taking this step helped me break down Nord’s UI interface, making it easy for me to discern what I would keep and what I would redesign for my rebranded version.

Identifying Interactions

Next I sought out to break down my task flow into the core micro interactions that occur when a user interacts with the interface through the completion of the primary task. This allowed me to gain further understanding about how the app actually flowed and provided me with inspiration on creating my own microinteractions at future steps of the process.

DESIGN

Part II

Picking a Brand Name

The first thing I did before I began rebranding Nord VPN was picking a new name for my app. This was an important step because this name would ultimately set the tone for my entire app and would have a significant impact about the design choices I would make.

In the end, I settled on CloudCover VPN for my rebranded app name. I choose this name because it was a clever analogy for what a VPN does(Gives you “cloudcover” from ISPs), and because I thought it would be fun to incorporate a weather motif into my designs and microinteractions.

Creating my UI Taskflow

Next, using Nord’s current interface and my rebranded app name as inspiration, I sketched out some preliminary UI designs for my rebranded app. I used these drawings as a launch pad to refine my designs in Figma towards my final UI pages that would be involved in my taskflow animation

Designing Microinteractions

After finalizing my UI, I started designing the actual microinteractions that would be present in my taskflow animation. While some micro interactions were purely utilitarian, I really tried to embrace the motif/style of my rebranded app to both give it more of a personality and make interacting with it memorable & unique

ANIMATION

Part III

Blocking out my Aninmation

After finalizing my designs in Figma, I then imported my layers over into After Effects in order to build my animation. My first step was simply blocking out my taskflow animation from start to finish. While this rough cut still needed to be tweaked and lacked proper timing, it gave me a solid starting point for future refinement.

Refining my Timing

After blocking out my animation, my next and final step was to go back through all my keyframes and adjust timing. Doing this made my animation smoother and ensured that the speed of the animation was never too slow or fast and that evrything looked more polished and real.

Final Thoughts

This project was a lot of fun for me for two reasons. Firstly, this was my first assignment where I wasn’t redesigning an existing website/app and I loved the freedom I was given to complete rebrand an app and make it my own.

Secondly, while using Adobe After Effects for the first time presented a lot of challenges for me, I was super excited begin to learn my way through the software and use it to create something awesome.In the end, I am very proud of my final Taskflow animation. It looks and flows very naturally, all while maintaining the artistic vision of what I set out to do with CloudCover VPN.
